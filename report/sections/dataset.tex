\subsubsection{TPTP.}
Two subsets of the TPTP problem library~\cite{Sut17} are used. The first is a
737-problem provable subset (FOF/THM), restricted to problems with 0--50 input
formulae, 0--150 atoms, and no equality or arithmetic. The second is a
204-problem counter-satisfiable subset (FOF/CSA) drawn from the same
complexity bounds, in which each problem is known to have no proof because
its negation is satisfiable. TPTP is the standard benchmark collection for ATP
research, with problems drawn from diverse mathematical domains (set theory,
geometry, algebra, synthetic logic) and assigned difficulty ratings by the CASC
competition infrastructure. Restricting to FOF and excluding equality and
arithmetic keeps both subsets within the ground-instantiation fragment
implemented here. The two subsets together test both sides of the prover: the
ability to find proofs and the ability to exhaust the search space and report
\textit{NotProvable}.

\subsubsection{FOLIO.}
The FOLIO dataset~\cite{han2024folio} consists of natural-language reasoning
problems in which human-authored premises about everyday scenarios are
formalised as FOF axioms and a conclusion is posed as a conjecture. The
validation split, comprising 280 generated FOF files (140 entailment/refutation
pairs), is included as a second source. FOLIO provides a structural contrast to
TPTP: problems arise from NLI annotation rather than mathematical proof, the
predicate vocabulary is drawn from natural language (e.g.\
\texttt{bornin}, \texttt{spendtimeplayingwith}), and the label distribution
includes true, false, and uncertain cases. This tests whether the prover's
behaviour generalises beyond abstract syntactic benchmarks to semantically
grounded, multi-premise problems of the kind that arise in formal NLI.

\subsubsection{AI-generated.}
To complement the above, 202 synthetically generated FOF problems in
TPTP format are included, one per file, across four tiers: \emph{easy} (32),
\emph{medium} (60), \emph{hard} (60), and \emph{expert} (50). The majority are
multi-formula files containing named axioms and a single conjecture.
Three families are used. \emph{Implication chains} assert $p_0(a)$ and a
sequence of $n$ universally quantified steps
$\forall X.\,(p_i(X)\Rightarrow p_{i+1}(X))$, with the conjecture $p_n(a)$;
$d$ structurally identical but irrelevant \emph{distractor} axioms of the same
form are added, increasing the number of applicable quantifier rules at each
proof step without contributing to any proof. Unprovable variants remove one
link from the chain. \emph{Syllogisms} encode a class hierarchy over ten named
predicates with a ground instance fact, testing multi-step universal
instantiation. \emph{Quantifier alternation} problems are single conjectures
drawn from classical $\forall/\exists$ validities and non-theorems: Drinker's
paradox, Russell's diagonal, the converse Barcan formula, and the (unprovable)
forward Barcan and $\exists\Rightarrow\forall$ inferences. Difficulty scales
across tiers by simultaneously increasing proof depth ($n \in [2,15]$) and
distractor count ($d \in [0,14]$). Because proof depth and distractor count are
fixed and known for each problem, the dataset provides a controlled setting in
which the effect of rule-selection quality and the step-budget cost of
iterative-deepening restarts can be studied in isolation.
